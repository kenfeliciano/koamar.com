I've always loved the tune [Face To Face](https://youtu.be/eUy9SXBQZNM?si=Be8YxBzvg9Q8CpUG) from Shakti's Natural Elements album, especially the groove in 15. They phrase it as I normally hear it, a bar of 4/4 followed by a bar of 7/8. I had an idea to do a tune in 15 but do it backwards with a bar of 7/8 followed by a bar of 4/4. That was the constraint for this tune. I suppose you could say it is in 7/8 and 8/8 so it's 7-8. Is that a thing kids?

I think I was inspired by some Allan Holdsworth tunes for the b9 #11 chords. Perhaps [Blues for Tony](https://youtu.be/FJy7PPcBlbI?si=mGXcf-OUzPvdlbpC)? Others? Anyway, that is not a normal chord for me to use.

I thought a guitar would be a good lead voice on this. But, my wife plays it on alto sax and that is nice too. Perhaps one of those tunes with both instruments in unison. Some harmony between the two instruments? Who knows.

You can solo over the A and B sections and/or just over the C section.

I started this in February 2022. I had the handwritten sketch of the A section on the piano for a long while. Sometime in January 2022, I opened up the version I had put into Finale (back when we had Finale) and I found that I had written a bridge. A few tweaks and suddenly the tune was done.

I didn't have a name for it until my wife asked why it had to be in 15. I said, "Because I Said So" and laughed. But then, we both thought, that's a good name for this tune!
